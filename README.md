#  📖 Process Introduction

This project involved training an image generation model based on the Pop Art style, using the StyleGAN2-ADA architecture. Building upon the official pre-trained model, I fine-tuned it with a small custom dataset to achieve stylized image generation.

#### Step 1

📃  [Image Download](https://git.arts.ac.uk/24006172/Critical-Coding-3-Finalproject/blob/main/Process/Download%20image%20for%20training.md)
📃  [Image_classifier](https://git.arts.ac.uk/24006172/Critical-Coding-3-Finalproject/blob/main/Process/Image_classifier.md)

The first step was preprocessing the dataset. I used the image download function from a previous class notebook to collect a number of Pop Art-style visual images from Pinterest, which served as the basis for my training dataset.

Initially, I resized the downloaded images and used them directly for training. However, I noticed that a significant portion of the Pop Art images featured prominent portraits. Therefore, I decided to further refine the dataset by isolating the portrait-style images into a separate subset for retraining.

During this process, I made use of the image classification model introduced in class and trained a small custom model to help filter and sort images specifically in the Pop Art portrait style. As a result, my final training dataset consisted entirely of Pop Art portrait images.


#### Step 2

📃  [Fine-tune](https://git.arts.ac.uk/24006172/Critical-Coding-3-Finalproject/blob/main/Process/Fine-tune%20a%20pre-trained%20GAN%20model.md)

After organizing the image dataset, I began fine-tuning the model using StyleGAN2-ADA. During the training process, I experimented with various combinations of pre-trained models and datasets. This included different variations of the Pop Art dataset categorized in multiple ways.I tested both the WikiArt.pkl and ffhq.pkl pre-trained models as the base for training.

After comparing the theoretical expectations with the actual outputs, I ultimately chose to proceed with the Pop Art portrait dataset in combination with the ffhq.pkl pre-trained model for the final round of training.


#### Step 3

📃  [Image Generation](https://git.arts.ac.uk/24006172/Critical-Coding-3-Finalproject/blob/main/Process/Image%20Generation.md)

After completing the model training, I used the official scripts to evaluate the model. I tested image generation using models saved at different training stages to observe the variations and issues that appeared as the training progressed.

Upon finishing the “final round of training”, I also experimented with image projection by inputting an image into the model to generate a corresponding output using the trained Pop Art-style model. However, the results did not meet my initial expectations. I believe achieving better outcomes in this area would require significantly more training time.

#  🔮 Future Develop

At this stage, my model has been trained up to "tick 266,kimg 1064.0". According to the official StyleGAN2-ADA GitHub repository, a full training cycle typically spans approximately 25,000 kimg. This means that my current model is still in the relatively early stages of training, having completed less than 10% of the recommended training iterations.

 At this stage, the generated images often exhibit noticeable artifacts, unstable stylistic consistency, or weak generalization. While some stylistic tendencies are beginning to emerge, fine facial features and background coherence are usually underdeveloped. This aligns with the results I have observed so far—although there is a noticeable improvement in the overall aesthetic toward Pop Art, the outputs still deviate from realism and display noise or a loss of detail.

 If training is continued to reach the full target of 25,000 kimg, the performance of the model is expected to improve significantly. This is evident by the noticeable gap between my current model output and the output generated by the official pre-trained model. However, this training experiment at least proves the feasibility of my overall approach. A process from dataset preparation to model fine-tuning proved to be practical and repeatable. With longer training times, I think the final visual results will be more in line with my initial expectations.
 
1.It‘s possible to build upon the current model by resuming training from the latest saved checkpoint. This approach is particularly effective when introducing a new dataset with similar stylistic qualities or modifying training parameters without starting from scratch. 

2.For small datasets, adaptive augmentation plays a critical role in maintaining model stability and avoiding overfitting. As more high-quality, stylistically consistent data is added, the model’s output can improve significantly in both coherence and diversity.
 
The above two ways are effective ways to continue to improve ink freshness that I will have the opportunity to try to use in the future, and these are the improvements I have analysed from[GAN2ADA GitHub](https://github.com/NVlabs/stylegan2-ada-pytorch).
